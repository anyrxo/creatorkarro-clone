## Lesson 1: The "AI Disclosure" Landscape

Platforms and governments are cracking down on undisclosed AI content. Transparency is key to longevity.

### Platform Rules (2024/2025)

1.  **Instagram/Meta**:
    - **Rule**: You must label AI-generated content if it looks photorealistic.
    - **How to Comply**: Toggle the "AI Label" switch when posting.
    - **Risk**: If you don't, Meta may automatically detect and label it for you (which looks worse) or shadowban your account.

2.  **TikTok**:
    - **Rule**: Mandatory labeling for realistic AI content.
    - **How to Comply**: Use the "AI-generated" toggle before posting.
    - **Risk**: Content removal or account suspension.

3.  **YouTube**:
    - **Rule**: Strict disclosure for realistic content.
    - **Risk**: Demonetization or strikes.

## Lesson 2: Copyright & Ownership

Who owns the images you generate?

### The Current Legal Stance (US/EU)
- **Raw AI Generations**: Generally **NOT copyrightable**. If a machine made it, you can't claim exclusive copyright over the raw image.
- **Edited/Modified AI Art**: If you significantly edit, paint over, or composite the image (Photoshop), you **CAN** claim copyright on the *human-created* elements.

### Protecting Your Brand
You can't copyright the *style*, but you can protect the **Character Identity**.
1.  **Trademark**: You can trademark the **Name** and **Logo** of your influencer.
2.  **Story/Lore**: You own the written backstory and captions.
3.  **LoRA/Model**: Your custom-trained LoRA is your trade secret. Don't share the file.

### Using Other People's Faces
**NEVER** train a LoRA on a specific celebrity or real person without permission.
- **Right of Publicity**: Celebrities have the right to control the commercial use of their likeness.
- **Deepfakes**: Creating non-consensual deepfakes (especially adult content) is illegal in many jurisdictions and will get you banned everywhere.
- **Best Practice**: Mix multiple faces or generate a completely unique face using AI to ensure your character doesn't look like a specific real person.

## Lesson 3: Taxes & Business Structure

Your AI influencer is a business. Treat it like one.

### Setting Up Your Entity
- **Sole Proprietorship**: Easiest to start, but personal liability.
- **LLC (Limited Liability Company)**: Recommended once you start making money. Protects your personal assets if your "influencer" gets sued.

### Banking
- **Separate Accounts**: Do not mix personal and business funds.
- **"High Risk" Categories**: Some banks or payment processors (Stripe/PayPal) may flag "adult" or "digital content" businesses.
    - *Solution*: Use creator-friendly banks or payment platforms specifically designed for creators (e.g., Cosmo, Paxum for adult content; standard banks for SFW brand deals).

### Tax Deductions
You can deduct business expenses:
- **Software Subscriptions**: Midjourney, ChatGPT, Adobe Creative Cloud.
- **Hardware**: That new RTX 4090 GPU? It's a business expense.
- **Outsourcing**: Payments to your VAs and editors.
- **Home Office**: A portion of your rent/internet.

## Lesson 4: Ethics & "Catfishing"

Don't be a scammer.

### The Golden Rule
**Never lie about being real.**
- Don't pretend to be a real human in DMs to trick people into sending money for "meetups" or "flights". That is fraud.
- You can maintain "kayfabe" (staying in character), but if asked directly, or in your ToS, clarify that it is a fictional character/entertainment service.

### Dating Apps
- **Tinder/Bumble**: Most ban AI profiles. Do not use them to funnel traffic; you will get banned.
- **AI-Specific Apps**: Use platforms designed for AI companions if that's your niche.
